Since the release of ChatGPT, I initiated a serious of research projects around LLM and generative AI. The projects inlcude

- Test Security [2020 - now]

Since 2020, I have been leading multiple data-driven projects funded by the Research Allocation and ETS test security initiative to innovate analytics and AI methods to support test security, especially for remotely administered tests. These projects have yielded several significant products, contributing to over 60% of cheating detection. Listed below are some of the tools we developed:
  - AutoESD - Automated Essay Similarity Detection: A system that identifies similar essays, presenting them to human experts for final decisions via an AI-assisted analytics dashboard.
  - AutoSSD - Automated Speech Similarity Detection is a system that detects similar speech responses and supply them through an analytics and AI assisted dashboard for human experts to make final decisions.
  - AI-Reviewer: AI-based essay/speech similarity reviewer is a machine learning system that utilizes a number of similarity features from essay/speech responses and human expert ajudications to make decisions automatically.
  - Keystroke analytics and biometrics include a set oft ools that are used to identify imposters,copywriting, and AI-generated essays.
  - Clickstream data-based suspicious behavioral detection includes a set of tools that identify anormly behaviors in test taking process.

- Assessment Process Data [2013 - now]

  I have been leading, co-leading, and participating in a variety of projects aimed at enhancing learning and assessment methods through the utilization of response process data. These projects encompass a range of innovative approaches, as detailed below:
  - CPS-rater: This project focuses on the automated annotation of communication in collaborative activities, streamlining the analysis of interactions in group settings.
  - Writing Analytics and Biometrics: Here,the emphasis is on leveraging keystroke data during the writing process. This approach allows for a nuanced understanding of writing habits and skills.
  - Learner Behavioral Analytics in Writing Mentor: This initiative analyzes user interaction with the online writing application, Writing Mentor, to gain insights into learning behaviors and patterns.
  - Winsight Process Data Analytics: thisproject employs process data analytics to support and enhance psychometric research and practices.
  - Analysis and modeling of Process Data from the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) to derive deeper insights into educational progress and trends.

Data Infrastructure and Software Packages
----------------------------------------
I’ve been spearheading a series of projects dedicated to developing data analytics solutions for telemetry logs from game-based assessments and digital learning environments. Listed below are some key projects:
- GlassPy: game log analysis in python [2014 - 2019]. I led this project to develop python package to address the analysis of log data analysis from games. Here is [a paper](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ets2.12096) from the project. A more recent presentation could be found in [this video](https://youtu.be/pcWXjS830fo).
- Data model for VPAs and Evidence Trace File [2014 - 2018]. I led this project to develop the ETS data model for virtual performance assessment and we proposed the evidence trace file to replace the log file. We also developed the evidence identification centered data design (EICDD) as an interface between ECD and data engineering. Here is [a paper](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ets2.12215) about the details of evidence trace file and our data model.
- EPCALanalytics[2019-2021].I led this project to develop analytics dashboards for the data from collaborative tasks deployed on the EPCAL platform.
- Many other public packages can be found on my [GitHub](https://github.com/jgbrainstorm)

Assessment of Collaboration, Communication and Team Dynamics
------------------------------------------------------------
I led/co-led a large number of research projects around assessments of collaborative problem solving and 21st century skills since 2013. The details are as follows:
- Collaborative Science Assessment Prototype (ECSAP) [2013 - 2017]. Leading this project to develop assessment prototype of collaborative problem solving in the domain of science. The prototype has been selected by Peggy Carr (NCES commissioner) as an example of future assessments in her presentation at the White House in 2014. Some findings from the project can be found in this [recent paper](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ets2.12276).
- ETS Platform for Collaborative Assessment and Learning (EPCAL) [2015 - now]. Leading this project to develop online platform for assessment and learning of human-human in- teractions, e.g., collaboration, communication, and social emotional learning. It is a pre- mium platform for conducting research on human-human interaction, and is supporting over 20 projects over the years funded by ETS and external funding agencies such as NSF, IES, ARI, Gates Foundation, and others. A description of the platform can be found in [this paper](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ets2.12181).
- ETS Platform for Collaborative Human Annotation (EPCHA) [2020 - now]. Leading this project to develop a distributed platform that simplify the human coding of the communication data from collaborative tasks.
- Collaborative Problem Solving Skills: Estimating an Individual’s Contribution to Small Group Per- formance [2019 - 2022]. Co-PI of this project funded by the [Army Research Institute](https://ari.altess.army.mil/) to investigate how to measure individual’s contribution to teamwork.
- Improve learning and assessments through human-human interaction[2020 - now]. Leading this multi-year project funded by ETS research allocation to explore ways of improving learning and assessment by using evidence from human-human interactions. [This paper](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ets2.12276) summarize the psychometric considerations and general scoring strategies for assessing collaborative problem solving.
- Developing Middle Grade Students Social Emotional Learning Skill through Technology En- hanced Collaborative Learning [2024-2028]. I am the co-PI of this project funded by the [Education Innovation Research initiative](https://www.ed.gov/news/press-releases/biden-harris-administration-announces-277-million-education-innovation-and-research-grants-help-address-academic-recovery) of the Department of Education. 


Astrophysics and Cosmology
--------------------------
- Dark Energy Survey [2009 - 2013]. 
I worked on the dark energy survey project while I was with Fermi National Accelerator Lab. The projects include:
  - [DECam focal plane flatness measurement](https://lss.fnal.gov/archive/2010/conf/fermilab-conf-10-191-a.pdf): I developed a optical method to measure the flatness of the focal plane of DECam.
  - [CCD image recuction and image quality pipeline](https://indico.cern.ch/event/129980/contributions/1351160/attachments/90580/129575/DECam_Hao_DPF2011.pdf): I developed the image reduction and image quality measurement pipeline for testing CCDs for DECam.
  - [Galaxy alignment measurement](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0004-637X/740/1/39): I performed the precision measurement of galaxy alignment in clusters based on SDSS data.

- Sloan Digital Sky Survey [2004 - 2009].
During my PhD, my research centers on developing unsupervised machine learning algorithm to detect cluster galaxies based on data from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. Some projects include: 
  - [Error corrected Gaussian Mixture Model](https://sites.google.com/site/jiangangecgmm/): I developed an extended gaussian mixture model that accounts for the measurement errors. Used this algorithm, we precisely measured the red sequence ridgeline evolution at different redshit.
  - [GMBCG algorithm](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0067-0049/191/2/254): I developed the Gaussian Mixture Bright Central Galaxy (GMBCG) algorithm and created the largest catalog of optical galaxy clusters as of 2013.
